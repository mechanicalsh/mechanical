Humanoids are about to live in homes and plants. The models that drive them are brilliant and jailbreakable. Mechanical builds the part that doesn't negotiate.

The thesis

Safety must be physical. A software constitution can be jailbroken. A silicon one cannot.

Every safety story in robotics today lives inside the same model that can be talked out of it. A clever prompt, a bad fine-tune, or a rushed update can switch it off, and nobody outside the company can tell. That is not a foundation you can put next to a child.

So we moved safety down a layer, into a core the reasoning model can never reach. The model proposes. The core decides whether any current reaches a motor. It is deterministic, formally verified, and on the wire, so the only way past it is to cut the robot off entirely, which is exactly the point.

We don't sell the circuit. Anyone can copy a safety co-processor in a few months. We sell the seal: the test, the certificate, and the mark that insurers and buyers come to demand. We aim to be the standard, the way UL, Dolby, and USB are standards. A standard, not a regulator. No permits, just adoption.

And we go fabless, the way ARM did. We own the governor and the identity root and the constitution. We let Thor and Qualcomm be the commodity muscle underneath. The day a robot near a person needs a reason to stop, that reason should be ours, and it should be a wire.

How we work

What we hold to.

  • Physical over promised

    If a guarantee can't be proven on a bench, we don't ship it as one.

  • Open where it counts

    The rules that stop a robot should be readable by anyone, not just us.

  • Standard, not gatekeeper

    We earn adoption. We don't demand permits or pick winners.

  • Own the seal, rent the FLOPS

    We hold the cert and the identity root, never the commodity compute.

The proving ground

The thesis, on camera, before the silicon.

A Unitree G1 in Isaac Sim where the safety core physically vetoes the robot's actuators in real time. It renders live on an RTX 4090 and streams to the browser. It is how we show the wire works while the die is still on the drawing board.

Today the G1 stands under gravity. Next comes a locomotion policy, then the veto layer cutting motions that would break the constitution, live.
