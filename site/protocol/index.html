The constitution

Plain rules. Signed once. Held everywhere.

The constitution is reviewable policy, not model weights. Force and speed caps, exclusion zones, allowed tools, step limits. Sign it, push it, and every unit inherits the same un-bypassable law.

What's in it

Three parts, one control plane.

01 / Constitution

The rules

Human-readable policy the safety core enforces. Versioned, signed, and diffable like any other source.

02 / Admin plane

The console

Fleet-wide roles, policy pushes, and a live veto log. The single pane for who can do what and what was stopped.

03 / Sequence

The identity

Every robot carries a hardware-rooted identity that signs its own logs and proves which constitution it runs.

Why open source

You can't trust a black box with the brake.

A rule that decides whether a robot near your kid moves or freezes should be one anyone can read. Open source is how the constitution earns trust, how the cert gets adopted, and how we become the standard instead of asking permission to be one.

  • [R]

    Readable

    The whole policy layer is auditable by anyone, not just us.

  • [P]

    Portable

    Run it on Mech silicon, your ASIL-D board, or in sim. Same rules.

  • [S]

    Standard

    Adoption-driven, like UL or USB. We define the cert, the market demands it.
