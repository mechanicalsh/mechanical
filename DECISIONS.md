Canonical record of locked decisions. Append, don't rewrite history.

## 2026-06-19 — Founding

**Name: Governor.** Locked after scoring ~60 candidates. The metaphor is the engine
governor — the mechanical limiter that physically caps an engine so it can't over-rev.
That *is* the product. Runners-up: Regent, Arbiter, Talos/Emet (golem pairing),
Takwin/Fitra (Islamic), Praetor. All beaten on thesis-fidelity. Accepting the
common-word trademark/SEO tax and choosing to out-execute it (Stripe/Square/Block
precedent).

**Company / chip = Governor. Open software layer = Govern Protocol.** Noun and verb,
hardware and software. The structural spine of the brand.

**Product thesis: safety must be physical.** Dual-brain by construction — reasoning
layer (any model the buyer picks) + a formally-verified safety governor with a hardware
veto over the actuators. Un-bypassable. We own the governor + identity root +
constitution, never the raw FLOPS.

**Go fabless (ARM model), not a fab.** Never out-NVIDIA NVIDIA on inference silicon —
that's the trap (the only path that would drain Robomart). Let Thor/Qualcomm be the
commodity muscle.

**Roadmap is a sequence, not a pick:** Govern OS → off-the-shelf safety co-processor
module → "Governor Inside" certified module → custom fabless governor die → license the
IP. Start one rung lower than the die: ship the physical veto on certified ASIL-D
silicon (AURIX / Cortex-R52) with zero tapeout.

**Wedge: don't beg the makers.** Interface only with open-SDK hardware first (Unitree
G1), define the cert from the software beachhead, earn design-ins, then move into
silicon from demand.

**Registry monetizes through insurance**, not as a standalone registry-startup
(registries are where startups die). Identity name "Sequence" and the registry name are
WIP.

**Daemon Protocol, Sentience Prize, Systema Robotica, SyRo Test = distribution /
flywheel, not products.** Keep cheap and alive; never monetize directly. They are the
unfair advantage that makes this governance play winnable.
